# Working with Custom Surfaces

**Last Updated:** 2026-01-10 **Difficulty Level:** Intermediate **Estimated
Time:** 45-60 minutes

## Learning Objectives

By the end of this tutorial, you will be able to:

- Design with custom countertop materials (exotic stone, concrete, wood)
- Create custom backsplash patterns and mosaics
- Work with custom cabinet finishes and specialty paints
- Design waterfall edges and custom countertop features
- Upload and apply custom material textures
- Calculate costs for custom fabrication
- Generate fabrication drawings for custom surfaces
- Collaborate with custom fabricators and craftspeople

## Prerequisites

- Completed [Creating Your First Kitchen Design](../beginner/first-design.md)
- Understanding of standard kitchen materials
- Existing kitchen design to customize
- Familiarity with material properties

## Introduction to Custom Surfaces

**Standard Materials:**

- Readily available from catalogs
- Fixed pricing and specifications
- Quick lead times (2-4 weeks)
- Limited customization

**Custom Materials:**

- Unique appearance and character
- Variable pricing based on complexity
- Longer lead times (4-12 weeks)
- Unlimited customization possibilities

This tutorial covers designing with custom surfaces that make your kitchen truly
one-of-a-kind.

### Step 2: Design a Waterfall Edge Countertop

One of the most popular custom features.

1. In your design, click on the **island countertop**
2. Click **"Edge Options"** in properties panel
3. Current: Standard 1.5" overhang, eased edge

[Screenshot: Standard island edge configuration]

4. Click **"Advanced Edge Design"**
5. Select **"Waterfall Edge"**

[Screenshot: Waterfall edge selector]

6. Configure waterfall:

   ```
   WATERFALL EDGE CONFIGURATION

   Which sides: [x] Left end  [ ] Right end  [x] Both ends
   Selected: Both ends

   Material continuation: Full thickness (3cm quartz)
   Seam location: Minimize visible seams
   Recommended: Mitered corner (45-degree seam)

   Cost calculation:
   Additional square footage: 18 sq ft (2 sides × 36" height × 30" depth)
   Material cost: 18 sq ft × $72/sq ft = $1,296
   Fabrication premium: $680 (complex miters, seams)
   Installation premium: $420 (precision required)

   Total waterfall edge cost: +$2,396
   Standard edge cost: $0
   Premium: +$2,396

   Lead time: Add 1 week for complex fabrication

   Visual impact: ★★★★★ (dramatic, modern)
   Difficulty: ★★★★☆ (requires skilled fabricator)
   ```

[Screenshot: Cost breakdown for waterfall edge]

7. Click **"Apply Waterfall Edge"**
8. The 3D view updates showing waterfall sides

[Screenshot: 3D view with waterfall edge island]

9. Toggle **"Show Seams"** to see miter locations
10. Seams appear as thin lines at the corners (barely visible when done well)

[Screenshot: Close-up of waterfall edge with seam indicators]

> **TIP:** Waterfall edges work best with materials that have veining or
> patterns that flow naturally (marble-look quartz, real marble, quartzite).

### Step 5: Design Concrete Countertops

Modern, industrial aesthetic.

1. Let's change the perimeter countertops to custom concrete
2. Click on **perimeter countertops**
3. Select **"Replace Material"** → **"Custom Concrete"**

[Screenshot: Concrete countertop configurator]

4. **Configure concrete mix:**

   ```
   CUSTOM CONCRETE COUNTERTOP DESIGN

   Concrete mix type: High-performance, fiber-reinforced
   Color: Charcoal gray (integral pigment)
   Aggregate: Exposed white marble chips (small)
   Texture: Smooth trowel finish

   Thickness: 2" (standard for concrete)
   Edge profile: Square edge with slightly rounded corner
   Finish: Sealed matte (food-safe sealer, 3 coats)

   Reinforcement: Steel rebar + fiber mesh
   Sink integration: Undermount (requires reinforced area)

   Embedded features:
   - [x] Integrated drainboard (grooves near sink)
   - [ ] Trivets (heat-resistant inserts)
   - [ ] Decorative inlays

   Cost calculation (42 sq ft perimeter):
   - Materials (concrete, pigment, aggregate): $720
   - Formwork & molds: $580
   - Fabrication (pouring, curing, grinding): $1,680
   - Sealing: $420
   - Installation: $680
   - Reinforcing: $280

   TOTAL: $4,360

   vs. Standard quartz (42 sq ft @ $72): $3,024
   Premium: +$1,336 (44% increase)

   Method: Precast (formed off-site, installed in sections)
   Alternative: Pour-in-place (+$800, 2 week on-site cure)

   Lead time: 6-8 weeks (cure time essential)
   Weight: ~250 lbs per 6-foot section (requires strong cabinets)
   ```

[Screenshot: Concrete sample with exposed aggregate]

## Custom Surface Best Practices

Remember these principles:

1. **Prioritize impact:** 2-3 custom elements > many small customizations
2. **Work with specialists:** Custom work requires specific expertise
3. **Plan lead times:** Custom materials take 2-3x longer than standard
4. **Get samples:** See and touch materials before committing
5. **Document everything:** Photos, drawings, specs prevent errors
6. **Understand maintenance:** Custom materials often need more care
7. **Budget contingency:** Add 15-20% buffer for custom work
8. **Get multiple quotes:** Custom pricing varies dramatically

## Common Custom Surface Mistakes

Avoid these pitfalls:

- **Unrealistic timelines:** Custom work takes time, plan accordingly
- **Mixing too many materials:** Stick to 3-4 max for cohesion
- **Ignoring maintenance:** Beautiful but impractical materials
- **Skipping samples:** Colors and textures look different in person
- **Inexperienced fabricators:** Custom work requires specific skills
- **Poor documentation:** Vague specs lead to wrong results
- **Trend-chasing:** Will you love that bold choice in 10 years?
- **Budget underestimation:** Custom work has hidden costs
