# Creating Your First Kitchen Design

**Last Updated:** 2026-01-10
**Difficulty Level:** Beginner
**Estimated Time:** 30-45 minutes

## Learning Objectives

By the end of this tutorial, you will be able to:

- Create a new kitchen design project from scratch
- Input basic room measurements and layout constraints
- Select and place essential kitchen elements (cabinets, appliances, countertops)
- Navigate the 3D design interface
- Save and share your first kitchen design
- Generate a basic cost estimate

## Prerequisites

- KitchenXpert account (free or paid tier)
- Basic measurements of your kitchen space
- A general idea of your preferred kitchen style (modern, traditional, etc.)
- No prior design experience required

## What You'll Need

Before starting, gather the following information:

- **Room dimensions:** Length, width, and ceiling height
- **Door and window locations:** Distance from corners and dimensions
- **Existing utilities:** Location of plumbing, electrical outlets, gas lines
- **Budget range:** Approximate total you want to spend (optional but helpful)

## Step-by-Step Instructions

### Step 1: Create a New Project

1. Log into your KitchenXpert dashboard at `https://app.kitchenxpert.com`
2. Click the **"+ New Project"** button in the top right corner
3. You'll see the "Create New Kitchen Design" dialog

[Screenshot: New Project dialog with empty form fields]

4. Fill in the project details:
   - **Project Name:** "My First Kitchen" (or any name you prefer)
   - **Location:** Select your country and region
   - **Project Type:** Choose "Full Kitchen Remodel"
   - **Timeline:** Select "Planning Phase"

5. Click **"Create Project"** to continue

[Screenshot: Completed project creation form]

> **TIP:** Choose a descriptive project name. You might create multiple designs, so names like "Modern Kitchen - White Cabinets" are more helpful than "Kitchen 1".

### Step 2: Enter Room Dimensions

1. After creating your project, you'll land on the **"Room Setup"** page
2. Choose your input method:
   - **Manual Entry** (recommended for beginners)
   - Photo Upload (for AI-assisted measurement)
   - Import Floor Plan

3. Select **"Manual Entry"**

[Screenshot: Room Setup page with input method options]

4. Enter your kitchen dimensions:
   - **Length:** 14 feet (4.27 meters)
   - **Width:** 12 feet (3.66 meters)
   - **Ceiling Height:** 9 feet (2.74 meters)

   *Note: These are example dimensions. Use your actual measurements.*

5. Click **"Next"** to continue

[Screenshot: Dimension input form with example values]

> **TIP:** Measure twice, design once! Accurate measurements are crucial. Measure from wall to wall, not baseboard to baseboard.

### Step 3: Add Doors and Windows

1. On the **"Openings"** page, you'll see your room outline
2. Click **"+ Add Door"**

[Screenshot: Empty room outline with Add Door and Add Window buttons]

3. Configure your door:
   - **Location:** Click on the wall where your door is located
   - **Distance from Left Corner:** 2 feet (0.61 meters)
   - **Door Width:** 3 feet (0.91 meters)
   - **Door Type:** Standard swing door
   - **Swing Direction:** Select inward or outward

4. Click **"Add Window"** to add a window
   - **Wall:** Select the wall (e.g., North wall)
   - **Distance from Left Corner:** 5 feet (1.52 meters)
   - **Width:** 4 feet (1.22 meters)
   - **Height from Floor:** 3 feet (0.91 meters)
   - **Window Height:** 3 feet (0.91 meters)

5. Add any additional windows or doors your kitchen has

[Screenshot: Room with door and window positioned]

6. Click **"Next: Utilities"** when done

> **WARNING:** Don't forget to mark door swing directions accurately. This affects where you can place cabinets and appliances.

### Step 4: Mark Utility Locations

1. On the **"Utilities"** page, mark existing infrastructure
2. Click **"+ Add Utility Point"**

[Screenshot: Utilities page with icons for different utility types]

3. Add your **sink plumbing**:
   - Click the **water drop icon**
   - Click on the wall where your sink plumbing is located
   - Distance from corner: 6 feet (1.83 meters)
   - Type: "Hot and Cold Water + Drain"
   - Mark as "Existing" (cannot be moved easily)

4. Add **electrical outlets**:
   - Click the **lightning bolt icon**
   - Click on walls where outlets are located
   - Typical kitchens have outlets every 4 feet along countertop walls
   - Height: 4 inches (10 cm) above counter height

5. Add **gas line** (if applicable):
   - Click the **flame icon**
   - Mark location if you have a gas stove
   - Distance from corner: 8 feet (2.44 meters)

6. Click **"Save & Continue"**

[Screenshot: Room with utility points marked]

> **TIP:** If you're planning to move plumbing or add new electrical, mark those as "Planned" instead of "Existing". This helps with cost estimates.

### Step 5: Choose Your Kitchen Layout

1. KitchenXpert will now suggest optimal layouts based on your room shape
2. You'll see options like:
   - **L-Shaped Layout** (recommended for your 12x14 room)
   - **U-Shaped Layout**
   - **Galley Layout**
   - **Island Layout**

[Screenshot: Layout options with 3D previews]

3. Click on **"L-Shaped Layout"** to select it
4. Review the preview showing suggested cabinet placement
5. Click **"Use This Layout"** to apply it

[Screenshot: L-shaped layout selected with cabinet zones highlighted]

> **TIP:** The L-shaped layout is ideal for small to medium kitchens. It maximizes counter space while maintaining an efficient work triangle between sink, stove, and refrigerator.

### Step 6: Select Your Cabinet Style

1. Navigate to the **"Cabinets"** tab in the left sidebar
2. Browse through available cabinet styles:
   - Modern Shaker
   - Traditional Raised Panel
   - Contemporary Flat Panel
   - Rustic Farmhouse

[Screenshot: Cabinet style gallery with thumbnails]

3. For this tutorial, select **"Modern Shaker - White"**
4. Click on the style to see available finishes
5. Choose **"Arctic White"** finish

[Screenshot: Cabinet style detail page showing finish options]

6. The system will automatically populate your layout with cabinets in this style

> **TIP:** You can mix cabinet styles later, but starting with one consistent style makes your first design easier.

### Step 7: Place Base Cabinets

1. Your layout now shows suggested base cabinet placement
2. In the 3D view, you'll see:
   - **Sink base cabinet** (36 inches wide)
   - **Standard base cabinets** (18-36 inches wide)
   - **Corner base cabinet** (36x36 inches)

[Screenshot: 3D view showing base cabinets in L-configuration]

3. To adjust a cabinet:
   - Click on any cabinet to select it
   - Use the **properties panel** on the right to modify:
     - Width
     - Depth (standard is 24 inches)
     - Drawer configuration
     - Door style

4. Example modification:
   - Click the cabinet next to the sink
   - Change width from 18" to 24"
   - Change type from "Single Door" to "3-Drawer Base"

[Screenshot: Cabinet properties panel with modification options]

5. Click **"Apply"** to save changes

> **WARNING:** Base cabinets must be 24 inches deep to accommodate standard countertops. Changing depth may create fitting issues.

### Step 8: Add Wall Cabinets

1. Click the **"Wall Cabinets"** button in the toolbar
2. The system suggests wall cabinets above each base cabinet
3. Click **"Auto-Place Wall Cabinets"** to add them all at once

[Screenshot: Wall cabinets automatically placed above base cabinets]

4. Standard wall cabinet specifications:
   - Height: 30 inches (or 36" for tall ceilings)
   - Depth: 12 inches
   - Bottom height: 18 inches above countertop

5. To manually add a wall cabinet:
   - Click **"+ Add Wall Cabinet"** in the toolbar
   - Click on the wall where you want to place it
   - Select width: 30 inches
   - Click **"Place"**

6. Adjust spacing:
   - Click on a wall cabinet
   - Use the **height adjuster** to move it up or down
   - Maintain 18" minimum clearance above countertops

[Screenshot: Wall cabinet height adjustment tool]

> **TIP:** For cabinets above the sink, consider using shorter 24" cabinets to accommodate the window.

### Step 9: Add Countertops

1. Click the **"Countertops"** tab in the left sidebar
2. Browse material options:
   - Granite
   - Quartz
   - Laminate
   - Butcher Block
   - Marble

[Screenshot: Countertop material library]

3. Select **"Quartz - Calacatta Marble Look"**
4. Choose edge profile: **"Eased Edge"** (simple, modern)
5. Click **"Apply to All Base Cabinets"**

[Screenshot: Countertop applied to L-shaped layout]

6. The system automatically:
   - Creates continuous countertop surfaces
   - Adds 1.5" overhang on exposed edges
   - Cuts sink opening in sink base location

7. To customize:
   - Click on the countertop surface
   - Adjust overhang: Change to 2" for bar seating
   - Add backsplash: Toggle "Include Backsplash" ON
   - Backsplash height: 18 inches (standard)

[Screenshot: Countertop customization panel]

> **TIP:** Quartz is a great beginner choice - it's durable, low-maintenance, and more affordable than natural stone.

### Step 10: Place Major Appliances

1. Click the **"Appliances"** tab in the left sidebar
2. You'll need these essential appliances:
   - Refrigerator
   - Range/Cooktop & Oven
   - Dishwasher
   - Microwave

[Screenshot: Appliance library with categories]

3. **Add a refrigerator:**
   - Click **"Refrigerators"** category
   - Select **"Standard French Door - 36" Width"**
   - Brand: Choose "Generic/Standard" for now
   - Click on your layout where you want to place it
   - Recommended: End of the L-shaped run

[Screenshot: Refrigerator being placed in design]

4. **Add a range:**
   - Click **"Ranges"** category
   - Select **"Freestanding Gas Range - 30" Width"**
   - Place it where your gas line is marked
   - The system ensures proper clearance from walls and cabinets

[Screenshot: Range positioned with clearance indicators]

5. **Add a dishwasher:**
   - Click **"Dishwashers"** category
   - Select **"Built-In Dishwasher - 24" Width"**
   - Place it next to the sink (within 36" for plumbing efficiency)
   - The system removes a base cabinet to make room

[Screenshot: Dishwasher placed next to sink base]

6. **Add a microwave:**
   - Click **"Microwaves"** category
   - Select **"Over-Range Microwave - 30" Width"**
   - It automatically positions above the range
   - Includes built-in ventilation

[Screenshot: Over-range microwave installed]

> **WARNING:** Maintain proper clearances:
> - 15" minimum between cooktop and wall cabinets
> - 18" recommended for safety
> - Over-range microwaves typically install at 66" from floor

### Step 11: Add Lighting

1. Click the **"Lighting"** tab in the left sidebar
2. For a basic kitchen, add:
   - **Recessed ceiling lights**
   - **Under-cabinet lighting**
   - **Pendant lights** (optional)

[Screenshot: Lighting options panel]

3. **Add recessed lights:**
   - Click **"Recessed Can Lights"**
   - Click **"Auto-Place Grid"**
   - Spacing: 4 feet apart
   - The system places 6-8 lights in a grid pattern

[Screenshot: Ceiling view showing recessed light placement]

4. **Add under-cabinet lighting:**
   - Click **"LED Strip Lights"**
   - Click **"Apply Under All Wall Cabinets"**
   - Color temperature: 3000K (warm white)
   - Brightness: 400 lumens per foot

[Screenshot: Under-cabinet lighting illuminated in 3D view]

5. Toggle the lighting preview:
   - Click the **sun/moon icon** in the toolbar
   - Switch between daylight and evening lighting
   - See how your kitchen looks at different times

> **TIP:** Under-cabinet lighting dramatically improves functionality and ambiance. It's one of the most impactful upgrades you can make.

### Step 12: Customize Colors and Finishes

1. Now that your layout is complete, refine the aesthetics
2. Click the **"Materials"** tab in the left sidebar
3. Review current selections:
   - Cabinets: Modern Shaker, Arctic White
   - Countertops: Calacatta Quartz
   - Backsplash: Same as countertop

[Screenshot: Materials summary panel]

4. **Change backsplash:**
   - Click **"Backsplash"** section
   - Click **"Choose Different Material"**
   - Select **"Subway Tile - 3x6 inches"**
   - Color: Bright White
   - Grout: Light Gray
   - Pattern: Classic horizontal brick

[Screenshot: Backsplash material selector]

5. **Add flooring:**
   - Click **"Flooring"** section
   - Select **"Luxury Vinyl Plank"**
   - Style: Light Oak
   - Plank width: 6 inches
   - Direction: Parallel to longest wall

[Screenshot: Flooring applied to kitchen design]

6. **Choose hardware:**
   - Click **"Cabinet Hardware"** section
   - Pulls: Modern Bar Pulls, 5" length
   - Finish: Brushed Nickel
   - Knobs: Not used (using pulls only)
   - Click **"Apply to All Cabinets"**

[Screenshot: Hardware selection with preview on cabinets]

> **TIP:** Stick to 2-3 main colors in your kitchen. White cabinets + wood floors + one accent color creates a cohesive, timeless look.

### Step 13: Review in 3D

1. Click the **"3D View"** button in the top toolbar (if not already active)
2. Use your mouse to navigate:
   - **Left-click + drag:** Rotate view
   - **Right-click + drag:** Pan view
   - **Scroll wheel:** Zoom in/out

[Screenshot: 3D view with navigation controls overlay]

3. Click the **"Walkthrough"** button to take a virtual tour
4. The camera will move through your kitchen showing:
   - Entry view
   - View from sink
   - View from cooking area
   - Overall room view

[Screenshot: Walkthrough mode with preset camera angles]

5. Try different visualization modes:
   - **Realistic:** Photo-realistic rendering
   - **Materials:** Focus on surfaces and textures
   - **Dimensions:** Shows measurements and spacing
   - **Wireframe:** Shows structural layout

[Screenshot: Side-by-side view modes comparison]

6. Click the **"Full Screen"** button for immersive view

> **TIP:** Take screenshots from different angles. These are helpful when discussing your design with contractors or family members.

## Common Issues and Solutions

**Issue:** Cabinets overlap with door swing
**Solution:** Select the cabinet and move it 3-4 inches away from the door path, or change door swing direction

**Issue:** Cannot place dishwasher next to sink
**Solution:** Delete the base cabinet in that location first, then place the dishwasher

**Issue:** Countertop doesn't look continuous at corner
**Solution:** Click the corner, select "Miter Joint" instead of "Butt Joint"

**Issue:** Budget seems too low/high
**Solution:** Check Settings > Location to ensure correct region. Labor costs vary significantly by area

**Issue:** 3D view is slow or choppy
**Solution:** Switch to "Performance Mode" in Settings > Graphics. Reduces detail but improves speed

## Tips for Success

1. **Measure carefully:** Use a laser measure for accuracy
2. **Follow the work triangle:** Sink, stove, and fridge should form a triangle with 4-6 foot sides
3. **Plan for traffic:** Allow 42-48 inches for walkways
4. **Consider workflow:** Place dishwasher near where you store dishes
5. **Don't forget outlets:** You'll need outlets every 4 feet along counters
6. **Think about lighting:** Plan for task lighting where you'll be working
7. **Save versions:** Before making big changes, save a version
8. **Start simple:** You can always add complexity later
