## Preamble

This Data Processing Agreement ("DPA") forms part of the Terms of Service ("Agreement" or "Principal Agreement") between KitchenXpert SAS ("Processor," "KitchenXpert," "we," "us") and the Customer ("Controller," "Customer," "you") who has agreed to the Terms of Service.

This DPA reflects the parties' commitment to comply with Regulation (EU) 2016/679 of the European Parliament and of the Council of 27 April 2016 on the protection of natural persons with regard to the processing of personal data and on the free movement of such data (the "GDPR"), and other applicable data protection laws.

This DPA shall be effective as of the date the Customer accepts the Terms of Service or begins using the Service, whichever occurs first.

In the event of any conflict between this DPA and the Principal Agreement, the provisions of this DPA shall prevail with respect to data protection matters.

## 1. Definitions

For the purposes of this DPA, the following terms shall have the meanings set forth below. Terms not defined herein shall have the meaning ascribed to them in the GDPR or the Principal Agreement.

**"Applicable Data Protection Law"** means all applicable laws and regulations relating to the processing of Personal Data, including the GDPR, the UK GDPR, the Swiss Federal Act on Data Protection, and any national implementing legislation.

**"Controller"** means the natural or legal person, public authority, agency, or other body which, alone or jointly with others, determines the purposes and means of the processing of Personal Data.

**"Data Subject"** means an identified or identifiable natural person whose Personal Data is processed under this DPA.

**"Instructions"** means the documented instructions issued by the Controller to the Processor directing the Processor to perform specific actions regarding Personal Data.

**"Personal Data"** means any information relating to an identified or identifiable natural person processed by the Processor on behalf of the Controller under this DPA.

**"Personal Data Breach"** means a breach of security leading to the accidental or unlawful destruction, loss, alteration, unauthorized disclosure of, or access to, Personal Data transmitted, stored, or otherwise processed.

**"Processing"** means any operation or set of operations performed on Personal Data, whether or not by automated means, such as collection, recording, organization, structuring, storage, adaptation, retrieval, consultation, use, disclosure, dissemination, restriction, erasure, or destruction.

**"Processor"** means a natural or legal person, public authority, agency, or other body which processes Personal Data on behalf of the Controller.

**"Sub-processor"** means any Processor engaged by KitchenXpert to process Personal Data on behalf of the Controller.

**"Supervisory Authority"** means an independent public authority established pursuant to the GDPR, including the CNIL in France.

## 4. Processor Obligations

KitchenXpert, as Processor, shall:

### 4.1 Processing on Instructions

Process Personal Data only on documented Instructions from the Controller, including with regard to transfers of Personal Data to a third country or international organization, unless required by Applicable Data Protection Law. If such a legal requirement applies, the Processor shall inform the Controller of that requirement before processing, unless prohibited by law.

### 4.2 Confidentiality

Ensure that persons authorized to process Personal Data have committed themselves to confidentiality or are under an appropriate statutory obligation of confidentiality. ### 4.3 Security Measures

Implement appropriate technical and organizational measures to ensure a level of security appropriate to the risk, as detailed in **Annex 2** to this DPA. These measures shall include, as appropriate:

- Pseudonymization and encryption of Personal Data

- Measures to restore availability and access to Personal Data in a timely manner following an incident

### 4.4 Sub-processors

Comply with the conditions set forth in Section 6 regarding the engagement of Sub-processors.

### 4.5 Assistance with Data Subject Rights

Assist the Controller, by appropriate technical and organizational measures, in fulfilling the Controller's obligations to respond to Data Subject requests, as set forth in Section 8.

### 4.6 Assistance with Security and Breach Notification

Assist the Controller in ensuring compliance with the obligations pursuant to Articles 32 to 36 of the GDPR, taking into account the nature of processing and the information available to the Processor.

### 4.7 Data Deletion or Return

At the choice of the Controller, delete or return all Personal Data to the Controller after the end of the provision of the Service, and delete existing copies unless Applicable Data Protection Law requires storage of the Personal Data.

### 4.8 Audits and Inspections

## 7. International Data Transfers

### 7.1 Processing Location

Personal Data shall be processed within the European Economic Area (EEA) where possible. The primary data processing location is AWS EU (Frankfurt, Germany).

### 7.2 Transfers Outside the EEA

Where Personal Data is transferred to Sub-processors located outside the EEA in countries without an adequacy decision, KitchenXpert shall ensure appropriate safeguards are in place, including:

- Standard Contractual Clauses (SCCs) approved by the European Commission
- Additional technical and organizational measures where necessary
- Assessment of the legal framework in the recipient country (Transfer Impact Assessment)

### 7.3 Standard Contractual Clauses

For transfers to Sub-processors in the United States and other non-adequate countries, KitchenXpert has entered into the Standard Contractual Clauses (Module 3: Processor to Processor) with each Sub-processor. Copies are available upon request.

### 7.4 Transfer Impact Assessments

KitchenXpert maintains Transfer Impact Assessments (TIAs) for transfers to non-adequate countries. These assessments evaluate the risks and supplementary measures in place. TIAs are available to the Controller upon request.

## 8. Data Subject Rights

### 8.1 Assistance to Controller

KitchenXpert shall assist the Controller in responding to requests from Data Subjects exercising their rights under Applicable Data Protection Law, including:

- Right of access
- Right to rectification
- Right to erasure
- Right to restriction of processing
- Right to data portability
- Right to object

### 8.2 Forwarding of Requests

If KitchenXpert receives a request directly from a Data Subject, KitchenXpert shall promptly forward such request to the Controller without responding to the Data Subject, unless legally required to do so.

### 8.3 Technical Measures

KitchenXpert shall implement technical measures to enable the Controller to fulfill Data Subject requests, including the ability to:

- Export Personal Data in a structured, commonly used format
- Delete Personal Data upon request
- Rectify inaccurate Personal Data

### 8.4 Response Timeframes

KitchenXpert shall respond to Controller requests for assistance within a reasonable timeframe, and in any event within ten (10) business days, to enable the Controller to meet its legal deadlines.

## 9. Personal Data Breach Notification

### 9.1 Notification to Controller

In the event of a Personal Data Breach, KitchenXpert shall notify the Controller without undue delay and in any event within forty-eight (48) hours of becoming aware of the breach.

### 9.2 Information to Provide

The notification shall include, to the extent available:

- A description of the nature of the breach, including categories and approximate number of Data Subjects and records affected
- The name and contact details of the data protection officer or other contact point
- A description of the likely consequences of the breach
- A description of measures taken or proposed to address the breach and mitigate its effects

### 9.3 Cooperation

KitchenXpert shall cooperate with the Controller in investigating the breach and fulfilling any notification obligations to Supervisory Authorities or Data Subjects.

### 9.4 Documentation

KitchenXpert shall document all Personal Data Breaches, including the facts relating to the breach, its effects, and the remedial actions taken.

## 10. Audits and Inspections

### 10.1 Audit Rights

The Controller has the right to audit KitchenXpert's compliance with this DPA, subject to the following conditions:

- Reasonable advance written notice of at least thirty (30) days
- Audits conducted during normal business hours
- Audits shall not unreasonably interfere with KitchenXpert's operations
- The Controller shall bear its own audit costs

### 10.2 Third-Party Auditors

The Controller may engage a qualified third-party auditor to conduct audits, provided that:

- The auditor is bound by confidentiality obligations
- The auditor is not a competitor of KitchenXpert
- The auditor's identity is approved by KitchenXpert (not to be unreasonably withheld)

### 10.3 Certification Reports

KitchenXpert shall make available relevant certification reports, including SOC 2 reports, to demonstrate compliance. Such reports may satisfy audit requirements at KitchenXpert's discretion.

### 10.4 Frequency

The Controller may conduct no more than one (1) audit per twelve (12) month period, unless a Personal Data Breach has occurred or required by a Supervisory Authority.

## Annex 2: Technical and Organizational Measures

### Physical Security

- Data centers operated by AWS with SOC 2 and ISO 27001 certification
- Restricted access to data center facilities
- Environmental controls (fire suppression, climate control, power redundancy)

### System Access Control

- Unique user authentication required
- Strong password policies enforced
- Multi-factor authentication for administrative access
- Automatic session timeouts
- Account lockout after failed attempts

### Data Access Control

- Role-based access control (RBAC)
- Principle of least privilege
- Regular access reviews
- Logging of all data access
- Segregation of duties

### Transmission Control

- TLS 1.3 encryption for all data in transit
- Certificate validation
- Secure API authentication
- VPN for administrative access

### Input Control

- Logging of data input, modification, and deletion
- User attribution for all changes
- Audit trails maintained
- Version control for designs

### Availability Control

- Regular automated backups
- Geographic redundancy (multiple availability zones)
- Disaster recovery procedures (RTO: 4 hours, RPO: 1 hour)
- Monitoring and alerting systems

### Separation Control

- Logical separation of customer data
- Separate environments for production, staging, and development
- Network segmentation
- Tenant isolation
