### What Are Cookies?

Cookies are small text files that are stored on your device (computer, tablet,
or mobile phone) when you visit a website. They are widely used to make websites
work more efficiently and provide information to website owners. Cookies enable
websites to recognize your device and remember information about your visit,
such as your preferences and settings.

### Why We Use Cookies

We use cookies and similar technologies to:

- Ensure the proper functioning of our Service
- Remember your preferences and settings
- Improve security and prevent fraud
- Analyze how you use our Service
- Personalize your experience
- Deliver relevant advertising (with your consent)

### Scope of This Policy

This Cookie Policy applies to cookies and similar technologies used on our
website and within the KitchenXpert platform. It does not cover third-party
websites linked from our Service, which have their own cookie policies.

## Managing Your Cookie Preferences

You have control over which cookies are placed on your device. Here are the ways
you can manage your preferences:

### Our Cookie Consent Banner

When you first visit our Service, you will see a cookie consent banner that
allows you to:

- Accept all cookies
- Reject non-essential cookies
- Customize your preferences by category

You can change your preferences at any time by clicking the "Cookie Settings"
link in the footer of our website.

### Browser Settings

Most web browsers allow you to control cookies through their settings. Here are
instructions for popular browsers:

**Google Chrome:**

1. Click the three-dot menu > Settings
2. Navigate to Privacy and Security > Cookies and other site data
3. Choose your preferred cookie setting

**Mozilla Firefox:**

1. Click the menu button > Settings
2. Select Privacy & Security
3. Under Cookies and Site Data, choose your preferences

**Apple Safari:**

1. Go to Safari > Preferences
2. Click the Privacy tab
3. Choose your cookie blocking preferences

**Microsoft Edge:**

1. Click the three-dot menu > Settings
2. Select Cookies and site permissions
3. Configure your cookie preferences

### Google Analytics Opt-Out

You can opt out of Google Analytics across all websites by installing the Google
Analytics Opt-out Browser Add-on: https://tools.google.com/dlpage/gaoptout

### Impact of Disabling Cookies

If you choose to disable cookies, please be aware that:

- **Essential cookies disabled**: The Service will not function properly. You
  may not be able to log in, save designs, or use core features.
- **Functional cookies disabled**: Your preferences will not be remembered
  between sessions. You may need to reconfigure settings each time you visit.
- **Analytics cookies disabled**: We will have less information to improve the
  Service, but your experience will not be affected.
- **Marketing cookies disabled**: You will still see advertisements, but they
  will not be personalized to your interests.

## Do Not Track Signals

### DNT Support

KitchenXpert honors "Do Not Track" (DNT) signals sent by your browser. When we
detect a DNT signal:

- We will not load non-essential cookies
- We will not track your browsing activity for advertising purposes
- Essential cookies required for the Service to function will still be set

### How to Enable DNT

**Google Chrome:** Settings > Privacy and Security > Send a "Do Not Track"
request

**Mozilla Firefox:** Settings > Privacy & Security > Send websites a "Do Not
Track" signal

**Apple Safari:** Safari does not have a built-in DNT setting but respects
cookie preferences

**Microsoft Edge:** Settings > Privacy, search, and services > Send "Do Not
Track" requests

## Similar Technologies

In addition to cookies, we may use similar tracking technologies:

### Local Storage

We use browser local storage to store certain preferences and cached data for
performance. Local storage data persists until explicitly cleared by you or our
application.

### Session Storage

Session storage is used for temporary data that is cleared when you close your
browser tab.

### Pixel Tags (Web Beacons)

Pixel tags are small transparent images used to track user interactions,
particularly in emails. They help us understand email open rates and
click-through rates.

### Device Fingerprinting

We do not use device fingerprinting for tracking purposes. Any device
information collected is used solely for security and fraud prevention.
